Questions 7–9 refer to the following information.

At \(27^{\circ}\text{C}\), five identical rigid \(2.0\) L vessels are filled with \(N_{2}(g)\) and sealed. Four of the five vessels also contain a \(0.050\) mol sample of \(NaHCO_{3}(s)\), \(NaBr(s)\), \(Cu(s)\), or \(I_{2}(s)\) as shown in the diagram above. The volume taken up by the solids is negligible, and the initial pressure of \(N_{2}(g)\) in each vessel is \(720\) mm Hg. All four vessels are heated to \(127^{\circ}\text{C}\) and allowed to reach a constant pressure.

 

Question 7

At \(127^{\circ}\text{C}\), the pressure in vessel 1 is found to be higher than that in vessel 2. Which of the following reactions best accounts for the observation?

(A) \(NaHCO_{3}(s)\rightarrow Na(s)+HCO_{3}(s)\)
(B) \(NaHCO_{3}(s)\rightarrow NaH(s)+CO_{3}(s)\)
(C) \(2~NaHCO_{3}(s)\rightarrow Na_{2}CO_{3}(s)+CO_{2}(g)+H_{2}O(g)\)
(D) \(2~NaHCO_{3}(s)+N_{2}(g)\rightarrow2~NaNO_{3}(s)+C_{2}H_{2}(g)\)
▶️ Answer/Explanation

1. Analyze the Observation:
Both vessels start with the same pressure of \(N_2(g)\). After heating, vessel 1 (\(NaHCO_3\)) has a higher pressure than vessel 2 (\(NaBr\)).

2. Inference:
By the Ideal Gas Law, pressure is proportional to moles (\(n\)) at constant V and T. The higher pressure must be due to an increase in the total moles of gas. This means \(NaHCO_3(s)\) decomposed to produce gas, while \(NaBr(s)\) did not.

3. Evaluate Options:
Only reaction (C) shows \(NaHCO_3(s)\) decomposing to produce gaseous products (\(CO_2(g)\) and \(H_2O(g)\)), which would increase the pressure.
✅ Answer: (C)

Question 8

At \(127^{\circ}\text{C}\), the entire sample of \(I_{2}\) is observed to have vaporized. How does the mass of vessel 5 at \(127^{\circ}\text{C}\) compare to its mass at \(27^{\circ}\text{C}\)? 

(A) The mass is less, since the \(I_{2}\) is in the vapor phase.
(B) The mass is the same, since the number of each type of atom in the vessel is constant.
(C) The mass is greater, since the \(I_{2}\) will react with \(N_{2}\) to form \(NI_{3}\), which has a greater molar mass.
(D) The mass is greater, since the pressure is greater and the particles have a higher average kinetic energy.
▶️ Answer/Explanation

1. Apply Law of Conservation of Mass:
The vessel is a sealed system. No matter can enter or leave.

2. Analyze the Process:
The change \(I_2(s) \rightarrow I_2(g)\) is a physical phase change (sublimation). The number of \(I\) atoms and \(N\) atoms inside the sealed vessel remains constant.

3. Conclusion:
Because the number of atoms of each element is constant, the total mass must also be constant, regardless of the temperature, pressure, or phase.
✅ Answer: (B)
