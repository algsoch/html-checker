Question 49

In a paper chromatography experiment, a sample of a pigment is separated into two components, X and Y, as shown in the figure above. [cite: 99, 100] [cite_start]The surface of the paper is moderately polar. [cite: 101] What can be concluded about X and Y based on the experimental results?
At t=30 min, the solvent (Hexane) has moved up, separating the pigment into Component Y (lower) and Component X (higher).]
(A) X has a larger molar mass than Y does. [cite: 102][cite_start]
(B) Y has a larger molar mass than X does. [cite: 103][cite_start]
(C) X is more polar than Y. [cite: 104][cite_start]
(D) Y is more polar than X. [cite: 105]
▶️ Answer/Explanation
Detailed solution

1. Identify Phases:
* [cite_start]Stationary Phase: The filter paper, which is "moderately polar". [cite: 101]
* [cite_start]Mobile Phase: The solvent, Hexane (\(C_6H_{14}\)), which is nonpolar. [cite: 97]
2. Principle of Chromatography: Separation occurs based on "like dissolves like."

  • Nonpolar components have a stronger affinity for the nonpolar mobile phase (Hexane) and will travel farther up the paper.
  • Polar components have a stronger affinity for the polar stationary phase (paper) and will "stick" to it, traveling a shorter distance.

3. Analyze Results:
* [cite_start]Component X traveled farther up the paper. [cite: 95] This means it is more nonpolar (more like Hexane).
* [cite_start]Component Y traveled a shorter distance. [cite: 96] This means it is more polar (more like the paper).
4. [cite_start]Conclusion: Component Y is more polar than component X. [cite: 105]
✅ Answer: (D)
